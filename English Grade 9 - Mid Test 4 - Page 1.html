I. Choose the word/phrase/sentence (A, B, C or D) that best fits the space or best answers the question given in each sentence.

1. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: A. park

In "park," the letter 'a' is pronounced as /ɑː/ (like in "car"). In the other words, 'a' is pronounced as /eɪ/ (a long 'a' sound): "cave" /keɪv/, "hate" /heɪt/, and "place" /pleɪs/.

2. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: B. tourist

In "tourist," the "ou" is pronounced as /ʊə/ or /ʊr/. In the other words, "ou" is pronounced as /aʊ/: "mountain" /ˈmaʊntən/, "outcrop" /ˈaʊtkrɒp/, and "cloudy" /ˈklaʊdi/.

3. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: C. nation

The word "nation" has the stress on the first syllable /ˈneɪ.ʃən/. The other words all have the stress on the first syllable: "picture" /ˈpɪk.tʃər/, "nature" /ˈneɪ.tʃər/, and "feature" /ˈfiː.tʃər/.

4. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: D. relax

The word "relax" has the stress on the second syllable /rɪˈlæks/. The other words all have the stress on the first syllable: "summit" /ˈsʌmɪt/, "outcrop" /ˈaʊtkrɒp/, and "scenic" /ˈsiːnɪk/.

5. Tourists should avoid making loud noises or getting too close to wild animals, as this can ________ them.

Correct answer: B. disturb

"Disturb" is the most appropriate word in this context. Loud noises or getting too close to wild animals can bother or upset them, which is what "disturb" means. "Protect" has the opposite meaning of what's needed, "spoil" (to ruin) doesn't fit the context of affecting animals, and "collect" (to gather) doesn't make sense in this sentence.

6. I really respect the ________ whose jobs are taking care of the national parks.

Correct answer: A. rangers

"Rangers" is the correct term for professionals who work in and take care of national parks. They are responsible for protecting wildlife, assisting visitors, and maintaining the park. "Climbers" are people who climb mountains as a sport, "security" refers to general safety personnel, and "lifeguards" supervise swimming areas.

7. We shouldn't ________; trash should be taken with us and put in bins.

Correct answer: D. litter

"Litter" means to drop trash carelessly and leave it, which is exactly what the sentence is advising against. "Waste" refers to using resources unnecessarily, "throw" is too general without specifying what's wrong with throwing, and "mess" typically refers to creating disorder rather than dropping trash specifically.

8. Hải ________ taking a trip to Dray Nur Waterfall, one of Vietnam's most impressive waterfalls.

Correct answer: A. decided

"Decided" fits best because it indicates that Hải made a choice to take the trip. "Agreed" would imply someone else proposed the idea first, "suggested" would mean Hải proposed it to someone else, and "wanted" doesn't clearly indicate that the trip is happening.

9. Among all the waterfalls in Vietnam, Bản Giốc Waterfall stands out as one of __________.

Correct answer: C. the tallest

When using the phrase "one of," it should be followed by a superlative form ("the tallest"). This indicates that Bản Giốc is among a group of very tall waterfalls. "The taller" would be used for comparing just two waterfalls, "as tall" would be used for equal comparison, and "a bit taller" doesn't fit grammatically after "one of."

10. In Cô Tô, travelers should consider __________ the charming fishing villages.

Correct answer: A. exploring

After the verb "consider," we typically use a gerund (verb + ing). So "exploring" is the correct form. "To explore" would not be grammatically correct after "consider," "explored" is a past participle that doesn't fit here, and "explore" as a bare infinitive doesn't work after "consider."

11. Deer Cave is __________ easier to reach than Son Doong Cave; tourists can take a boat trip instead of trekking all day.

Correct answer: D. much

"Much" is the correct intensifier to use with the comparative "easier." It indicates a significant difference in how easy it is to reach Deer Cave compared to Son Doong Cave. "More" would be redundant with "easier" (which already includes the comparative meaning), "as" would be used for equal comparison, and "bit" is grammatically incorrect (should be "a bit" if used).

12. Pù Luông National Park, __________ is located in the north-central region of Vietnam, is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

Correct answer: B. which

"Which" is the correct relative pronoun for a non-defining relative clause that provides additional information about Pù Luông National Park. "Where" would be used for referring to a place within the park, "that" is typically used for defining clauses (without commas), and "whose" indicates possession, which doesn't fit here.

13. Nam: "I think the local council should limit the number of visitors to Deer Cave to protect its ecosystem." Tuấn: "__________ but it might have a negative impact on the local economy."

Correct answer: C. I understand what you're saying

"I understand what you're saying" is the most appropriate response because Tuấn partially agrees with Nam but is going to present a counter-argument (the negative economic impact). It shows understanding before introducing a "but" clause. "I can't agree with you more" and "I think you're right" would show complete agreement, which contradicts the "but" clause that follows. "I completely disagree" is too strong and doesn't match with acknowledging Nam's point.

14. Anh: "I want to do something different on our next trip. How about going trekking in Sapa?" Mai: "__________ The scenery there is breathtaking."

Correct answer: A. That's not a bad idea.

"That's not a bad idea" is the correct response because Mai follows with a positive comment about the scenery being breathtaking, indicating agreement with Anh's suggestion. "I don't think so" and "I hate going trekking" would contradict the positive comment about the scenery. "What can we do in Sapa?" wouldn't make sense followed by a statement about the scenery.

II. Look at the sign or the notice. Choose the best answer (A, B, C or D) for questions 15 and 16.

IN CASE OF FIRE

DO NOT USE ELEVATOR

15. What does the sign mean?

Correct answer: B. For your safety, do not use the elevator during a fire.

The sign clearly states "IN CASE OF FIRE DO NOT USE ELEVATOR," which is a safety warning instructing people not to use elevators during fire emergencies. Option B correctly interprets this as a safety directive. Option A incorrectly suggests the elevator itself is on fire, option C misinterprets the graphic, and option D incorrectly suggests using the elevator is optional rather than prohibited.

THE SWIMMING POOL

OPENS AT 6:30 A.M.

EXCEPT SUNDAYS

WHEN IT OPENS AT 7 A.M.

16. What does the notice mean?

Correct answer: D. The opening hour of the swimming pool on Sunday is 7 a.m.

The notice states that the swimming pool opens at 6:30 a.m. every day EXCEPT Sundays, when it opens at 7 a.m. Option D correctly states this information. Option A is incorrect because the pool doesn't open at 6:30 on Sundays. Option B is incorrect because the pool opens later (not earlier) on Sundays. Option C is incorrect because the pool opens earlier (not later) during weekdays.

III. Choose the word (A, B, C or D) that best fits each space in the following passage.

Hey everyone! This is my first blog post! I want to share some cool things I love (17) ________ in my free time! I hope my blog makes you think about starting some new hobbies. Books are my best friends! I love getting lost in strange worlds and learning new things from different stories. (18) ________ I'm not reading, I enjoy playing the guitar. I started taking lessons last year, and now I can play several songs!

17.

Correct answer: A. doing

The gerund "doing" is the correct form after the verb "love" when referring to activities we enjoy. The phrase "things I love doing in my free time" is a complete expression about hobbies and activities. "Do" (present tense) would be incorrect grammatically, "to do" (infinitive) is possible but less natural in this context, and "done" (past participle) doesn't make sense here.

18.

Correct answer: A. When

The word "When" is the appropriate conjunction here as it indicates time - specifically the time when the writer is not reading, they enjoy playing guitar. "Although" would suggest a contrast that doesn't make sense in this context. "Because" would incorrectly suggest that not reading is the reason for playing guitar. "If" would make this a conditional statement, which doesn't fit the intended meaning.
