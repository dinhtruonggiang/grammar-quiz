I. Choose the word/phrase/sentence (A, B, C or D) that best fits the space or best answers the question given in each sentence.

1. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: B. guide

In "guide," the "ui" is pronounced as /aɪ/ (like in "ride"), while in "fruit," "juice," and "suit," the "ui" is pronounced as /uː/ (like in "boot"). This makes "guide" the word with the different pronunciation.

2. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: A. chemical

In "chemical," the "ch" is pronounced as /k/, while in "cheerful," "chapter," and "children," the "ch" is pronounced as /tʃ/ (the "ch" sound as in "chair"). This makes "chemical" the word with the different pronunciation.

3. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: A. litter

The stress patterns are:

  • "litter" - /'lɪtə/ (stress on first syllable)
  • "decide" - /dɪ'saɪd/ (stress on second syllable)
  • "maintain" - /meɪn'teɪn/ (stress on second syllable)
  • "create" - /kri'eɪt/ (stress on second syllable)
"Litter" has stress on the first syllable, while all the other words have stress on the second syllable.

4. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: B. impressive

The stress patterns are:

  • "teenager" - /'tiːneɪdʒə/ (stress on first syllable)
  • "impressive" - /ɪm'presɪv/ (stress on second syllable)
  • "nutrient" - /'njuːtriənt/ (stress on first syllable)
  • "calorie" - /'kæləri/ (stress on first syllable)
"Impressive" has stress on the second syllable, while all the other words have stress on the first syllable.

5. A __________ breakfast provides essential nutrients to start the day.

Correct answer: D. nourishing

"Nourishing" is the best choice as it directly relates to providing nutrients, which is mentioned in the sentence. A nourishing breakfast is one that supplies the body with essential nutrients. The other options don't fit as well:

  • "Fatty" has negative connotations and doesn't necessarily suggest essential nutrients
  • "Natural" refers to food without artificial ingredients but doesn't specifically address nutritional content
  • "Detox" refers to removing toxins rather than providing nutrients

6. Please turn off your phone so that it doesn't __________ the animals during our safari adventure.

Correct answer: C. disturb

"Disturb" is the correct choice because phone noises and lights can startle or annoy animals. The other options don't make logical sense in this context:

  • "Disappear" doesn't fit as phones don't make animals vanish
  • "Damage" is too strong for the effect of phone noise on animals
  • "Remove" doesn't make sense in this context

7. Students can benefit __________ PE lessons as they promote physical fitness.

Correct answer: A. from

"Benefit from" is the correct preposition collocation with the verb "benefit." We use "from" to indicate the source of the benefit or advantage. The other prepositions don't collocate correctly with "benefit" in this context:

  • "Benefit with" is not a standard English collocation
  • "Benefit to" is used differently (as in "this is a benefit to someone")
  • "Benefit on" is not a standard English collocation

8. Litter can __________ the beauty of even the most picturesque landscapes.

Correct answer: B. spoil

"Spoil" is the correct answer because it means to damage or ruin something, making it less attractive or enjoyable. Litter "spoils" the beauty of landscapes by making them less visually appealing. The other options don't fit as well:

  • "Break" suggests physically breaking something, which isn't appropriate for beauty
  • "Transfer" means to move something from one place to another
  • "Hit" means to strike something physically, which doesn't work with beauty

9. If you want to maintain your weight, you should __________ eating too much fast food.

Correct answer: A. stop

"Stop" is the most appropriate verb when talking about ceasing an unhealthy habit like eating too much fast food. The other options don't fit the context as well:

  • "Deny" means to declare something untrue or to refuse to admit something
  • "Refuse" is typically used when rejecting something offered to you, not for personal habits
  • "Disagree" means to have a different opinion, which doesn't make sense with eating habits

10. __________ artificial lake in the world is the Kariba Dam, which is located on the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe.

Correct answer: B. The largest

"The largest" is correct because the sentence is making a superlative statement about the Kariba Dam being the biggest artificial lake in the world. The other options are grammatically or contextually incorrect:

  • "The large" is grammatically incorrect; it should be "The large lake" if used
  • "As large as" is used for comparisons, but there's nothing being compared here
  • "Much large" is grammatically incorrect; it should be "much larger" if used

11. Alicia doesn't mind __________ up early to enjoy the peaceful morning atmosphere.

Correct answer: D. getting

"Getting" is correct because after the expression "don't mind," we use the gerund form (-ing). This is a fixed grammar pattern in English. The other forms are incorrect in this context:

  • "Got" is past tense and doesn't fit after "doesn't mind"
  • "To get" (the infinitive) is not used after "doesn't mind"
  • "Get" (base form) doesn't work grammatically after "doesn't mind"

12. I wish I had __________ as I wanted to explore all the tourist destinations of Vietnam.

Correct answer: D. much more time

"Much more time" is correct because the context suggests the person feels they needed significantly more time than they had. The phrase "much more" emphasizes the greater quantity needed. The other options have issues:

  • "As many time" is grammatically incorrect as "time" is uncountable and doesn't use "many"
  • "More time" would be grammatically correct but lacks the emphasis of "much more"
  • "As much time" would fit in a comparative structure ("as much time as...") but doesn't work in this wish statement

13. Annie: "Would you mind helping me plan my trip to Vietnam?"
Thảo: "__________"

Correct answer: A. Of course not! I'd love to help.

When someone asks "Would you mind...?", the correct way to agree to help is to say "No" or "Of course not" (meaning "I don't mind") followed by an expression of willingness. Option A correctly does this. The other options are inappropriate:

  • Option B misunderstands the question completely
  • Option C doesn't answer Annie's request for help
  • Option D starts correctly with "Not at all" but then asks about the trip which doesn't make sense since Annie is still planning it

14. Nancy: "Why don't you go swimming once or twice a week?"
Tony: "__________, but swimming's not really my thing."

Correct answer: C. Thanks for the idea

"Thanks for the idea" is the most polite and appropriate response to acknowledge Nancy's suggestion before explaining why Tony doesn't want to follow it. The other options don't fit with Tony's follow-up statement:

  • "I can't agree with you more" contradicts Tony's statement that swimming isn't his thing
  • "I think so" suggests agreement, which contradicts not liking swimming
  • "I hope you're right" doesn't make sense in response to a suggestion about swimming

Correct answer: A. You mustn't turn left.

The sign shown is a "No Left Turn" traffic sign, which prohibits drivers from making a left turn at that location. It consists of a left turn arrow with a red circle and diagonal line across it, which is the universal symbol for prohibition. The other options misinterpret the sign:

  • Option B suggests only some vehicles need to stop, which is incorrect
  • Option C suggests the opposite (that you must turn left), which contradicts the sign
