II. VOCABULARY AND GRAMMAR (3.2 points)

Choose the best option (A, B, C or D) to complete each of the following questions.

5. We're going to head _____ to the beach for a picnic this afternoon.

Correct answer: B. out

"Head out" is a phrasal verb meaning to leave or depart from somewhere, especially to begin a journey. This is the appropriate expression when going to the beach for a picnic.

6. They used their _____ to buy a new refrigerator.

Correct answer: A. savings

"Savings" refers to money that has been saved over time, which is what people typically use to make large purchases like refrigerators. The other options don't make sense in this context.

7. While on vacation, we always _____ some souvenirs for our friends and family back home.

Correct answer: C. pick up

"Pick up" is the correct phrasal verb to use when talking about buying or getting souvenirs while on vacation. It means to purchase or acquire something casually while you're somewhere.

8. Setting ambitious goals can help you _____ yourself and reach new heights.

Correct answer: D. challenge

"Challenge" is the correct verb because ambitious goals push you to go beyond your current abilities. "Restrict," "discourage," and "comfort" all have negative or passive connotations that don't match the positive context of the sentence.

9. One bad experience shouldn't put you _____ from trying new things.

Correct answer: B. off

"Put off" is a phrasal verb meaning to discourage or deter someone from doing something. This fits the context of not letting a bad experience stop you from trying new things.

10. The company hired _____ workers to help during the busy holiday season.

Correct answer: D. temporary

"Temporary" is the correct answer because holiday seasons are short periods when businesses need extra help. Once the busy season ends, these workers are no longer needed, making their employment temporary rather than permanent, long-term, or fixed.

11. Despite his busy schedule, Mark remains _____ and always finds time to pursue his hobbies.

Correct answer: A. easy-going

"Easy-going" describes someone who is relaxed and not easily upset or stressed, which fits with the context of Mark being able to balance his busy schedule with his hobbies. "Laid-back" has a similar meaning but "easy-going" is the best match for this context.

12. He said he _____ Australia the following week.

Correct answer: A. would visit

This is an example of reported speech for a future action. In direct speech, he might have said "I will visit Australia next week." When converted to reported speech, "will" changes to "would" and "next week" changes to "the following week."

13. She advised him _____ early.

Correct answer: D. not to leave

After the verb "advise," we typically use the structure "not to + infinitive" when giving negative advice. The correct form is "not to leave" rather than the other options, which are grammatically incorrect in this context.

14. John said it was the most delicious dish he _____.

Correct answer: C. had tried

The past perfect tense "had tried" is correct because we're referring to an action (trying the dish) that happened before another past action (John saying it was delicious). When referring to a past experience before a point in the past, we use past perfect.

15. "You ought to revise more for your exams." My teacher _____ us _____ more for our exams.

Correct answer: D. told / to revise

When reporting advice, we use "told + object + to + infinitive." The teacher told us (object) to revise (infinitive) more. The verb "said" would require "that" and wouldn't take the infinitive form.

16. Emily asked _____.

Correct answer: D. who I talked to

In reported questions, we change the word order from question to statement form. The direct question might have been "Who did you talk to?" In reported speech, this becomes "who I talked to" without the auxiliary verb and with the subject before the verb.

17. He keeps _____ the same mistakes.

Correct answer: C. making

After the verb "keep" in the sense of continuing to do something, we use the -ing form (gerund). "He keeps making the same mistakes" indicates that this is an ongoing action that continues to happen.

18. How long _____ badminton?

Correct answer: C. have you been playing

This question requires the present perfect continuous tense because we're asking about an activity that started in the past and continues to the present. "How long have you been playing badminton?" is the correct form, with the auxiliary verb "have" before the subject in a question form.

19. I _____ him for 10 years now.

Correct answer: A. have known

The present perfect tense "have known" is correct because it refers to an action that started in the past and continues to the present. The phrase "for 10 years now" indicates this ongoing time period. "Know" is a stative verb that is not typically used in continuous forms, which is why we use "have known" rather than "have been knowing."

20. I don't mind _____ the book for a few days.

Correct answer: A. if you borrow

After the phrase "I don't mind," we typically use a conditional clause with "if" followed by a present tense verb when giving permission. "If you borrow" is the correct form to express this conditional permission.

23. My mistake _____ me a lot of time and money.

Correct answer: D. lost

The verb "lost" is appropriate here because the mistake caused the person to waste both time and money. We can say something "lost me time/money" when it causes us to waste or spend unnecessarily.

24. It was really inconvenient and I was very _____ with myself.

Correct answer: D. annoyed

The context indicates that the person was upset about their mistake. "Annoyed" best captures this feeling of irritation or frustration with oneself. The other options don't make sense in this negative context.

25. I had a morning flight back home the next day, so I checked the flight time on my ticket, _____ the alarm on my phone and went to sleep.

Correct answer: B. set

The correct collocation is "to set an alarm." We use "set" when programming an alarm to go off at a specific time. The other verbs are not used with alarms in this way.

26. As soon as the alarm _____ off, I got up and hurried to the airport.

Correct answer: C. went

The phrasal verb "go off" is used to describe an alarm making a sound when it activates. "The alarm went off" is the correct collocation.

27. I'd thought it said 8.00 a.m. but in fact, when I _____ my glasses on, I could see it said 3.00 a.m.

Correct answer: B. put

The phrasal verb "put on" is used with glasses. We say "put on glasses" to describe the action of placing glasses on your face.

28. I had to wait _____ eight hours in the airport for the next flight and I had to pay for a new ticket.

Correct answer: D. another

"Another" is the correct word when referring to an additional period of time. "Another eight hours" means "eight more hours." The other options don't fit grammatically in this context.

V. WRITING (1.6 points)

1. Choose the letter A, B, C or D to indicate the sentence that is CLOSEST in meaning to each of the following questions.

33. Jack's industrious nature led him to start his own successful business at a young age.

Correct answer: C. Jack's hardworking character motivated him to establish his own successful business early in life.

"Industrious" means hardworking, so "hardworking character" in option C is the closest synonym. "Motivated" is also close to "led" in meaning, and "establish" is similar to "start." The phrases "early in life" and "at a young age" also have equivalent meanings.

34. He asked if I had ever done yoga before.

Correct answer: A. He wanted to know if I had tried yoga in the past.

"Asking if I had ever done yoga before" is most closely paraphrased as "wanting to know if I had tried yoga in the past." The other options change the meaning of the original question.

35. Sarah's hard work finally paid off when she received a promotion at work.

Correct answer: A. Sarah's efforts were rewarded when she earned a promotion at work.

"Hard work paid off" means that her efforts were eventually rewarded, which is exactly what option A states. The other options either contradict the original statement or add information that isn't in the original sentence.

36. Advertising yourself is not important if you have talent.

Correct answer: C. True talent can attract attention naturally.

The original statement suggests that talent is more important than self-promotion, which is closest to the idea that "true talent can attract attention naturally." The other options either contradict or significantly change the meaning of the original statement.

37. Quality / customer service / the hotel / improve / after / numerous / complaints / guests.

Correct answer: B. The quality of customer service at the hotel improved after numerous complaints from guests.

This option is grammatically correct and arranges the given words in a logical order. It uses the past simple tense "improved" which is appropriate for a completed action in the past. Option B also correctly uses "the quality" with the definite article.

Correct answer: A. Europe's rich culture and history attract millions of tourists each year.

This option is grammatically correct. It uses the correct possessive form "Europe's" and the plural verb "attract" which agrees with the compound subject "culture and history." 39. Gain / valuable / job experience / essential / secure / higher / salary.

Correct answer: D. Gaining valuable job experience is essential for securing a higher salary.

This option is grammatically correct. "Gaining" is the correct gerund form to use as the subject of the sentence, and "securing" is the correct gerund form after the preposition "for." Options A, B, and C all have grammatical errors.

Correct answer: B. Many students engage in volunteer work during their summer breaks.

This option is grammatically correct. The correct collocation is "engage in" (not "engage" or "engage on"), and "many students" (not "many of students") is the correct form. Option B correctly arranges all the given words into a grammatical sentence.
