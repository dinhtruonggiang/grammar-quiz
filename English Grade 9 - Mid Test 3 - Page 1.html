I. Choose the word/phrase/sentence (A, B, C or D) that best fits the space or best answers the question given in each sentence.

1. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: B. rule

In "rule," the underlined 'u' is pronounced as /uː/ (long u sound), while in "hunt," "jungle," and "culture," the underlined 'u' is pronounced as /ʌ/ (short u sound).

2. Which word has the underlined part pronounced differently from that of the others?

Correct answer: C. reduced

In "reduced," the ending "-ed" is pronounced as /t/, while in "suggested," "finished," and "impressed," the ending "-ed" is pronounced as /d/ or /ɪd/.

3. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: D. maintain

"Maintain" has the stress on the second syllable (main-TAIN), while "litter," "wonder," and "damage" have the stress on the first syllable (LIT-ter, WON-der, DAM-age).

4. Which word has a different stress pattern from that of the others?

Correct answer: A. consider

"Consider" has the stress on the second syllable (con-SID-er), while "government," "national," and "animal" have the stress on the first syllable (GOV-ern-ment, NA-tion-al, AN-i-mal).

5. The ____________ of Fansipan offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Correct answer: B. summit

"Summit" refers to the highest point of a mountain, which would offer spectacular views. "Roof," "head," and "cap" are not commonly used to describe the top of a mountain in this context.

6. The tropical ____________ in Vietnam, particularly Phú Mát National Park, are famous for their diversity of plants and animals.

Correct answer: A. rainforests

Vietnam is known for its tropical rainforests, particularly in national parks. The climate and geography of Vietnam do not support deserts or extensive grasslands, and "safari parks" are human-made attractions.

7. Tourists can enjoy a peaceful boat ride that ____________ them through the scenic Trà Sư Melaleuca Forest.

Correct answer: C. floats

"Floats" is the most appropriate verb to describe how a boat carries passengers through water. "Swims" is for living beings, "falls" indicates dropping, and "slips" suggests accidental movement.

8. Adventurous hikers often go ____________ the paths in search of hidden waterfalls or breathtaking views.

Correct answer: D. off

"Go off the paths" means to leave the established trails and explore less traveled areas, which is what adventurous hikers would do to find hidden waterfalls.

9. Sơn Đoòng Cave is considered as one of ____________ caves in the world.

Correct answer: A. the largest

When referring to something that ranks at the top in a category (one of a group), we use the superlative form "the largest." Sơn Đoòng is actually considered the largest cave in the world by volume.

10. If we don't stop ____________, our natural environment will suffer serious damage.

Correct answer: C. littering

After "stop," we use the gerund form of the verb ("littering"). "To litter" would be used after verbs like "want," "plan," etc. "Litter" alone is incorrect, and "littered" is the past tense form.

11. The Amazon rainforest, ____________ is the oldest rainforest on Earth, is home to an incredible variety of wildlife.

Correct answer: D. which

We use "which" to introduce a non-defining relative clause that provides additional information about the Amazon rainforest. "That" is used for defining clauses, while "it" and "there" don't work grammatically in this context.

12. Travelers should ____________ visiting natural wonders during peak seasons.

Correct answer: B. avoid

"Avoid" is the most appropriate verb meaning "to keep away from." "Refuse" means to reject an offer, "prevent" means to stop something from happening, and "disagree" means to have a different opinion.

13. Bảo: "I think we should go hiking in Cát Tiên National Park this weekend. The weather is supposed to be perfect!" Lan: "____________"

Correct answer: A. That sounds amazing.

This response shows enthusiasm and agreement with Bảo's suggestion. The other options don't make sense as responses: B repeats information, C suggests something Bảo has already proposed, and D sounds uncertain.

14. An: "Let's go to Đà Nẵng by plane. It's faster." Phương: "____________ as it's cheaper and we can enjoy the scenery along the way."

Correct answer: C. I think going by train is better

Based on the explanation that follows (it's cheaper and offers scenery), Phương clearly prefers the train over the plane, so "I think going by train is better" is the logical choice.

III. Choose the word (A, B, C or D) that best fits each space in the following passage.

The rise of English
The story of the English language is amazing. The language did not exist two thousand years ago. By 500 CE, there was a language called Englisc. (17) ____________ only about thirty thousand people spoke it. Nearly one thousand years later, at the time of Shakespeare, English was the language of about six million people. Most of them lived in Britain. (18) ____________ is the countries of England, Scotland and Wales. But English did not even reach the edges of Britain, because many people spoke other languages in Scotland and Wales.

17.

Correct answer: B. However

The sentence needs a contrasting conjunction. "However" correctly shows the contrast between the existence of the language and the small number of people who spoke it. "Although" would require a different sentence structure, "Therefore" indicates a result, and "Because" indicates a reason, neither of which fits the context.

18.

Correct answer: C. Which

"Which" is the correct relative pronoun to introduce additional information about Britain. The sentence defines what constitutes Britain (England, Scotland, and Wales). "What" would be used for definitions, "Where" for locations, and "That" typically for defining (rather than non-defining) clauses.
