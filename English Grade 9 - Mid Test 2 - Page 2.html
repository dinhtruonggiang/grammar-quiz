19. Choose the best word to fill in the blank:

Correct answer: B. on

We use the preposition "on" with specific days and dates. In this case, "on New Year's Eve" is the correct expression for referring to that specific holiday. Other prepositions don't work here:

  • "at" - typically used for specific times or locations, not for days
  • "with" - indicates accompaniment or means, not appropriate for time references
  • "for" - indicates purpose or duration, not appropriate here

20. Choose the best word to fill in the blank:

Correct answer: D. what

The expression "to see what it was like" is the correct form when someone wants to discover the nature or quality of something. The context is about experiencing the city center during New Year's Eve.

  • "that" - would not work grammatically in this structure
  • "how" - could work in some contexts but less appropriate than "what" for describing an experience
  • "when" - refers to time, which doesn't fit the context
  • "what" - correctly completes the expression "to see what it was like" which refers to discovering the nature/quality of the experience

21. Choose the best word to fill in the blank:

Correct answer: B. packed

The sentence describes the streets filled with many people during New Year's Eve. "Packed" is the most appropriate word here, as it specifically describes a large number of people crowded in a space.

  • "full" - can describe a crowded place but is less vivid and specific than "packed"
  • "packed" - best describes a place extremely crowded with people
  • "stuck" - implies inability to move but doesn't necessarily indicate a crowd
  • "tight" - can describe cramped spaces but is less common for describing streets with crowds
"The streets were packed with people" is a common collocation to describe crowded streets.

22. Choose the best word to fill in the blank:

Correct answer: A. As

The word "As" is the correct choice because it introduces a clause describing something happening simultaneously with another action. The narrator is thinking about the future while watching the fireworks.

  • "As" - correctly indicates "while" or "at the same time as" - the person was thinking while watching
  • "So" - would indicate a result or consequence, which doesn't fit here
  • "During" - would need to be followed by a noun, not a clause with subject and verb
  • "Before" - would indicate the thinking happened prior to watching the fireworks, which contradicts the meaning
"As we were watching" correctly shows the simultaneous nature of the two actions.

V. Use the correct form of the word given in each sentence.

29. Students should be taught about the _______ of balanced nutrition. (IMPORTANT)

Correct answer: importance

From the root word "IMPORTANT" (adjective), we need to form a noun to complete the sentence. The correct noun form is "importance" which means "the quality of being important." The phrase "the importance of balanced nutrition" is grammatically correct and makes sense in this educational context.

30. _______ if parents do exercise regularly, children are more likely to develop this healthy habit. (NATURE)

Correct answer: Naturally

From the root word "NATURE" (noun), we need to form an adverb that can start a sentence. The correct form is "Naturally," which is used to indicate something happens in a normal or expected way. In this context, it means "as would be expected" or "of course." The sentence structure suggests we need an adverb at the beginning to modify the entire statement.

31. _______ meals are essential for a healthy body and mind. (NOURISH)

Correct answer: Nourishing

From the root word "NOURISH" (verb), we need to form an adjective to describe "meals." The correct adjective form is "nourishing" which means "providing nutrients needed for growth and good health." The present participle form of the verb "nourish" functions as an adjective in this sentence to describe what kind of meals are essential.

32. Some people find it helpful to keep a food diary to track their _______ habits. (EAT)

Correct answer: eating

From the root word "EAT" (verb), we need to form a noun that can describe "habits." The gerund form "eating" works as a noun in this context. "Eating habits" is a common collocation that refers to the way a person typically consumes food - what, when, and how they eat. The sentence structure requires a noun that can function as an adjective before "habits."

33. Eating a _______ diet is important for overall health and fitness. (BALANCE)

Correct answer: balanced

From the root word "BALANCE" (noun or verb), we need to form an adjective to describe "diet." The correct form is "balanced" (the past participle of the verb "to balance" used as an adjective). A "balanced diet" is one that contains the proper proportions of food categories needed for good health. The sentence structure requires an adjective before the noun "diet."

34. The artist decided to _______ the painting with a few final touches. (ENHANCE)

Correct answer: enhance

The root word "ENHANCE" is already a verb, and we need a verb form after "to" in this sentence. The base form "enhance" (meaning "to improve or add to the quality or value of something") is the correct form following the infinitive marker "to." In this structure, we need the base form of the verb without any changes.

VII. Rewrite each of the following sentences in another way so that it means almost the same as the sentence printed before it.

37. Although the woman is old, she still exercises every day.

→ Despite ______________________________________.

Correct answer: Despite being old, the woman still exercises every day.

To rewrite this sentence starting with "Despite," we need to change the clause structure:

  • Original: "Although the woman is old, she still exercises every day."
  • Rewritten: "Despite being old, the woman still exercises every day."
The conjunction "Although" introduces a subordinate clause with a subject and verb ("the woman is old"). When we replace it with "Despite," we need to use a gerund construction ("being old") because "despite" is a preposition that must be followed by a noun or noun phrase, not a clause.

38. The doctor suggested that Tommy should drink plenty of water every day.

→ The doctor advised ______________________________________.

Correct answer: The doctor advised Tommy to drink plenty of water every day.

To rewrite this sentence using "advised" instead of "suggested," we need to change the clause structure:

  • Original: "The doctor suggested that Tommy should drink plenty of water every day."
  • Rewritten: "The doctor advised Tommy to drink plenty of water every day."
The verb "suggest" can be followed by a that-clause with "should," but the verb "advise" is typically followed by an object (Tommy) and an infinitive with "to" (to drink). Both verbs have different grammatical patterns despite having similar meanings.

39. In spite of being on a diet, Kate ate a big slice of chocolate cake at the party.

→ ALTHOUGH ______________________________________.

Correct answer: Although Kate was on a diet, she ate a big slice of chocolate cake at the party.

To rewrite this sentence starting with "Although," we need to change the preposition phrase to a clause:

  • Original: "In spite of being on a diet, Kate ate a big slice of chocolate cake at the party."
  • Rewritten: "Although Kate was on a diet, she ate a big slice of chocolate cake at the party."
"In spite of" is a complex preposition followed by a gerund phrase ("being on a diet"). When we replace it with "Although," we need to form a full subordinate clause with a subject and verb ("Kate was on a diet"). Note that we also need to repeat the subject "she" in the main clause.

40. "Why don't we grab some lunch at the cafeteria today?" Alex told us.

→ SUGGESTED ______________________________________.

Correct answer: Alex suggested (that) we (should) grab some lunch at the cafeteria that day.

To rewrite this sentence using "suggested," we need to change the direct speech to reported speech:

  • Original: ""Why don't we grab some lunch at the cafeteria today?" Alex told us."
  • Rewritten: "Alex suggested (that) we (should) grab some lunch at the cafeteria that day."
This transformation requires several changes:
  1. Change "Why don't we" (which is a suggestion) to "suggested (that) we (should)"
  2. Change "today" to "that day" (time shift in reported speech)
  3. Change the subject-verb order (from "Alex told us" to "Alex suggested")
  4. Remove the question marks and quotation marks
Note: Both "suggested that we grab" and "suggested that we should grab" are grammatically correct.
