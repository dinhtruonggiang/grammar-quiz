19. I hope I __________ able to cook really well one day.

Correct answer: B. will be

The sentence expresses a hope about the future, so we need a future tense. "Will be" is the correct form to express a future state. "Going to" would be incomplete without "be", "would be" is used for hypothetical situations, and "going to be" would work grammatically but is not the best option because "will be" is more commonly used with hopes about the future.

20. I __________ pictures of everything – plants, animals, the clouds.

Correct answer: D. take

The correct collocation with "pictures" is "take pictures." We "take" photos or pictures with a camera. "Make pictures" would refer to creating drawings or artwork, "see pictures" means to view them, and "get pictures" could mean receiving them from someone else.

21. I've played both instruments for a long time, __________ I was about six.

Correct answer: B. since

"Since" is used to indicate the starting point of an action that continues to the present. The writer started playing instruments at age six and continues to play them. "For" is used with periods of time (e.g., "for ten years"), not with a specific point in time. "After" would mean they only started playing after age six, and "before" would incorrectly suggest they played before they were six.

22. I'm learning Spanish and French as well __________ English.

Correct answer: C. as

The phrase "as well as" is used to add information, meaning "in addition to." It tells us the writer is learning Spanish and French in addition to English. The other prepositions ("to," "for," "from") don't form correct expressions in this context.

29. Tam Cốc Cave is famous for its _____________ boat ride through the rice paddies. (SCENERY)

Correct answer: scenic

The word "SCENERY" needs to be changed to its adjectival form "scenic" to describe the boat ride. We need an adjective before the noun "boat ride." "Scenery" is a noun that refers to the view or landscape itself, while "scenic" describes something related to beautiful scenery.

30. Phuket is _____________ accessible with its international airport and well-developed public transportation. (EASE)

Correct answer: easily

The word "EASE" needs to be changed to its adverbial form "easily" to describe how Phuket can be accessed. We need an adverb to modify the adjective "accessible." "Ease" is a noun, while "easily" is the adverb form used to describe how something is done.

31. _____________ to An Bang beach often enjoy its peaceful atmosphere and stunning sunsets. (TRAVEL)

Correct answer: Travelers

The word "TRAVEL" needs to be changed to "Travelers" as we need a noun that refers to people who travel. The sentence requires a subject at the beginning, and "Travelers" fits as the people who would go to An Bang beach.

32. The most _____________ thing about Cái Bè floating market is the boats full of colorful fruits and vegetables. (INTEREST)

Correct answer: interesting

The word "INTEREST" needs to be changed to its adjectival form "interesting" to describe "thing." We need an adjective that describes what quality the thing has. "Interest" is a noun, while "interesting" is the adjective that describes something that causes interest.

33. Sometimes it's difficult to make a(n) _____________ on where to go for your next holiday. (DECIDE)

Correct answer: decision

The word "DECIDE" needs to be changed to its noun form "decision," as we need a noun after the indefinite article "a(n)." "Decide" is a verb, while "decision" is the noun form that refers to the choice or determination that is made.

34. Our neighbors are very _____________ and welcoming. (FRIEND)

Correct answer: friendly

The word "FRIEND" needs to be changed to its adjectival form "friendly" to describe the neighbors. We need an adjective after "very" to describe what kind of neighbors they are. "Friend" is a noun, while "friendly" is the adjective that describes someone who behaves in a kind way.

38. It took us about two hours to reach the summit of Mount Langbiang.

→ We SPENT

Correct answer: about two hours reaching/climbing the summit of Mount Langbiang

To rewrite the sentence using "SPENT," we need to change the structure to "We spent [amount of time] [gerund]..." So "We spent about two hours reaching the summit of Mount Langbiang" or "We spent about two hours climbing to the summit of Mount Langbiang" would be correct answers.

39. My sister likes exploring new cities more than relaxing on the beach.

→ My sister PREFERS

Correct answer: exploring new cities to relaxing on the beach

When using "PREFERS" to show a comparison of preference between two activities, the structure is "prefer [something] to [something else]." So "My sister prefers exploring new cities to relaxing on the beach" correctly expresses the original meaning.
