18. Choose the word that best fits the space (18):

Correct answer: B. where

The correct answer is "where" because we need a relative adverb that refers to a place. The context suggests "large plastic bubbles" will be places in which people can live and breathe, so "where" is the appropriate word to introduce this information. "Where" is used to introduce a relative clause that specifies a location.

19. Choose the word that best fits the space (19):

Correct answer: C. like

The word "like" is used to make a comparison. In this context, the sentence is saying we need to find another planet that has similar characteristics to Earth. "Like" is the most appropriate word to express this similarity.

20. Choose the word that best fits the space (20):

Correct answer: D. too

The correct answer is "too" because the sentence is explaining that Mercury and Venus are excessively hot, making them uninhabitable for humans. "Too" indicates an excessive amount that causes a negative consequence (in this case, being unable to live there). The structure "too + adjective + for + someone" is commonly used to express that something exceeds acceptable limits.

21. Choose the word that best fits the space (21):

Correct answer: B. Unfortunately

The word "Unfortunately" is the most appropriate because the sentence introduces a negative situation or problem - the fact that even if we find a suitable planet, it would be very far away. "Unfortunately" is an adverb used to express regret or disappointment about a situation, which matches the tone of this sentence as it describes an obstacle to finding a habitable planet.

22. Choose the word that best fits the space (22):

Correct answer: A. get

The verb "get" is the most appropriate in this context because it's a general verb of movement that can be used for reaching a destination by any means. Since we're talking about space travel to another planet, specific verbs like "drive" or "ride" are too limited and not suitable. "Come" would be more appropriate if the perspective was from someone already at the destination. "Get" is versatile and commonly used in the phrase "get there" to mean reaching a destination.

IV. Use the correct form of the word given in each sentence.

29. It's essential for young people to keep a balanced diet and get enough sleep to stay _______________. (HEALTH)

Correct answer: healthy

The correct form is "healthy" - an adjective formed from the noun "health." In this sentence, we need an adjective to describe the state of the young people who maintain a balanced diet and get enough sleep. "Healthy" describes the positive physical condition that results from these good habits.

30. The _______________ of stalactites and stalagmites in Sơn Đoòng cave took thousands of years. (FORM)

Correct answer: formation

The correct form is "formation" - a noun derived from the verb "form." The sentence requires a noun to describe the process by which stalactites and stalagmites were created or developed over time. "Formation" refers to the process of something being formed or the resulting structure.

31. Our bones _______________ become weaker as we're older. (NATURE)

Correct answer: naturally

The correct form is "naturally" - an adverb formed from the noun "nature." In this sentence, we need an adverb to describe how bones become weaker. "Naturally" indicates that this weakening is a normal part of the aging process and happens as a result of natural biological processes rather than external factors.

32. Tourists should be aware of the _______________ of traveling to mountainous regions during rainy seasons. (DANGEROUS)

Correct answer: dangers

The correct form is "dangers" - the plural noun form of the adjective "dangerous." The sentence requires a noun to complete the phrase "be aware of the..." and since we're talking about multiple potential hazards of traveling in mountainous regions during rainy seasons (such as landslides, flash floods, difficult terrain), the plural form "dangers" is appropriate.

33. It's really _______________ when people talk loudly on their phones while I'm trying to enjoy the sightseeing tour. (ANNOY)

Correct answer: annoying

The correct form is "annoying" - an adjective formed from the verb "annoy." In this sentence, we need an adjective to describe the situation of people talking loudly on phones. "Annoying" describes something that causes irritation or mild anger, which fits the context perfectly.

34. We spent our vacation _______________ exploring beaches and cafés. (LEISURE)

Correct answer: leisurely

The correct form is "leisurely" - an adverb formed from the noun "leisure." In this sentence, we need an adverb to describe how they explored beaches and cafés. "Leisurely" means in a relaxed, unhurried manner, which appropriately describes a vacation activity.

VI. Rewrite each of the following sentences in another way so that it means almost the same as the sentence printed before it.

37. The Amazon Rainforest is larger than any other rainforest in the world.

→ The Amazon Rainforest is the __________________________________________________.

Correct answer: largest rainforest in the world

This question requires changing a comparative structure (larger than any other) to a superlative structure (the largest). When something is larger than all others in a category, it is the largest. The complete rewritten sentence should be "The Amazon Rainforest is the largest rainforest in the world."

38. Though kale tastes bitter, it is full of antioxidants that promote good health.

→ Despite __________________________________________________.

Correct answer: its bitter taste, kale is full of antioxidants that promote good health

This question requires rewriting the sentence using "Despite" instead of "Though." "Though" and "Despite" both express contrast, but they have different grammatical structures. "Though" is used with a clause, while "Despite" is used with a noun or gerund. The rewritten sentence should be "Despite its bitter taste, kale is full of antioxidants that promote good health."

39. Shennongjia is located in Hubei Province in China. It is famous for its rich biodiversity.

→ WHICH __________________________________________________.

Correct answer: is located in Hubei Province in China, Shennongjia is famous for its rich biodiversity

This question requires combining two separate sentences using a relative clause with "which." The relative pronoun "which" refers to Shennongjia and connects the two pieces of information. The rewritten sentence should be "Shennongjia, which is located in Hubei Province in China, is famous for its rich biodiversity." However, since the prompt wants to start with "WHICH," the answer should be "Which is located in Hubei Province in China, Shennongjia is famous for its rich biodiversity."

40. The boys denied damaging any of the exhibits in the museum.

→ DENIED __________________________________________________.

Correct answer: having damaged any of the exhibits in the museum

This question requires changing the verb form after "denied" from a gerund ("damaging") to a perfect gerund ("having damaged"). When denying an action that supposedly happened in the past, the perfect gerund is often used. The complete rewritten sentence should be "The boys denied having damaged any of the exhibits in the museum."
