III. Choose the word that best fits each space in the following passage (continued).

— she was a scientist — said that, (17)____________ all these websites talking about detox, it's a myth that you can eat certain things to do that. Our organs actually detox naturally.

She also said that there are some (18)____________ which are good for you – I didn't know that! She suggested eating salad with olive oil or having nuts, for example, because these things protect our organs.

There was also a guy on the program who tried only eating junk food for a month, and he said that he felt terrible. The scientist said that this kind of food is not really (19)____________, although it gives you lots of calories. The problem is that it is low in vitamins and other things which our bodies need.

At the end of the program, she said that it was important for governments to (20)____________ healthy living — for example, by stopping companies advertising candy and soda during children's TV programs. But, at the end of the program, her (21)____________ was that we all need to do a lot more to stay healthy, because so (22)____________ children in countries all around the world are obese.

17. Choose the word that best fits space (17).

Correct answer: C. although

The context shows a contrast between "all these websites talking about detox" and the scientist's assertion that "it's a myth" that you can eat certain things to detox. Let's analyze each option:

  • "despite" - followed by a noun phrase, not a clause; would require "despite all these websites..."
  • "in spite" - similar to "despite", requires "of" following it; not grammatical in this context
  • "although" - introduces a subordinate clause and shows contrast; can come at the beginning of a sentence
  • "though" - means the same as "although" but is more informal; could work, but "although" is more appropriate in formal writing
"Although" is the best choice because it properly introduces the contrasting clause "all these websites talking about detox" before the main point that "it's a myth".

18. Choose the word that best fits space (18).

Correct answer: B. nutrients

The context discusses foods that are good for you and protect your organs. Let's analyze each option:

  • "calories" - refers to energy content of food; doesn't match with the examples of salad with olive oil or nuts as protective foods
  • "nutrients" - substances that provide nourishment essential for growth and health; matches with the examples given
  • "fats" - while some healthy fats exist in olive oil and nuts, the generic term "fats" doesn't encompass all beneficial elements in these foods
  • "vitamins" - specific type of nutrients; too narrow for the context that includes various beneficial compounds
"Nutrients" is the most appropriate term because it broadly covers all beneficial substances in foods like olive oil and nuts that help protect our organs.

19. Choose the word that best fits space (19).

Correct answer: C. nourishing

The context discusses junk food and why it makes people feel terrible. The sentence structure indicates that this food is "not really [word]" followed by "although it gives you lots of calories." Let's analyze each option:

  • "natural" - refers to foods from nature, without processing; doesn't fit the contrast with calorie content
  • "harmful" - would make the "although" clause illogical; if it's harmful, high calories wouldn't be a contrasting positive
  • "nourishing" - means providing nutrients needed for growth and health; creates the logical contrast: not nourishing despite having calories
  • "dangerous" - too extreme, and again wouldn't make sense with the "although" clause
"Nourishing" is the best choice because it creates the appropriate contrast: junk food has calories (energy) but lacks the nutrients that make food truly nourishing for the body.

20. Choose the word that best fits space (20).

Correct answer: B. promote

The context discusses what governments should do regarding healthy living. Let's analyze each option:

  • "gain" - doesn't collocate naturally with "healthy living" in this context
  • "promote" - means to support or encourage something; fits well with government actions to encourage healthy living
  • "maintain" - suggests keeping something at the same level; doesn't fit with initiating new policies
  • "protect" - while governments might protect citizens, the phrase "protect healthy living" is not a natural collocation
"Promote" is the best choice because it matches the action of governments taking steps to encourage and support healthy living habits, such as regulating advertising.

21. Choose the word that best fits space (21).

Correct answer: D. conclusion

The context discusses what happened "at the end of the program." Let's analyze each option:

  • "introduction" - refers to the beginning, not the end of something
  • "organization" - refers to structure or an entity; doesn't fit the context of wrapping up a program
  • "summary" - while a summary can occur at the end, the phrase "her summary was that..." is less common than "her conclusion was that..."
  • "conclusion" - refers to the final part or judgment; naturally collocates with "at the end of the program"
"Conclusion" is the best choice because it properly describes the final judgment or point made at the end of the program.

22. Choose the word that best fits space (22).

Correct answer: C. a lot of

The context refers to the number of children who are obese worldwide. Let's analyze each option:

  • "much" - used with uncountable nouns; "children" is countable, so this is incorrect
  • "many" - used with countable nouns; grammatically correct with "children" but the phrase "so many" often implies comparison
  • "a lot of" - can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns; "so a lot of" isn't grammatically correct though
  • "a few" - indicates a small number, which contradicts the concerning tone about obesity levels
The most likely answer is B. "many" because it correctly modifies the countable noun "children" and works with "so" to form "so many" - a common expression indicating a large quantity that's concerning.

V. Use the correct form of the word given in each sentence.

29. Some diets can be _____________ to your health, so you should ask for advice from an expert before starting any new diet. (HARM)

Correct answer: harmful

We need to form an adjective from the noun "HARM" to describe how diets can affect your health negatively.

  • "HARM" (noun) → "harmful" (adjective) by adding the suffix "-ful"
  • Other forms: "harmless" (adjective, meaning causing no harm), "harmfully" (adverb)
"Harmful" correctly describes something that can cause damage or injury to health, which fits the context of dangerous diets.

30. It's important to eat _____________ to maintain good physical and mental well-being. (HEALTH)

Correct answer: healthily

We need to form an adverb from "HEALTH" to describe how one should eat.

  • "HEALTH" (noun) → "healthy" (adjective) → "healthily" (adverb)
  • The adverb form is created by adding "-ily" to the adjective "healthy" (dropping the "y" and adding "ily")
  • The adverb is needed here because it's modifying the verb "eat"
"Healthily" correctly describes the manner of eating that promotes good health.

31. Make sure your daily meals contain enough _____________, such as vitamins, minerals, and fiber. (NUTRITION)

Correct answer: nutrients

We need a plural noun derived from "NUTRITION" to refer to the beneficial substances in food.

  • "NUTRITION" (noun, uncountable) → "nutrient" (noun, countable) → "nutrients" (plural)
  • While "nutrition" refers to the process of nourishing, "nutrients" refers to the specific substances that provide nourishment
  • The examples given (vitamins, minerals, fiber) indicate we need the plural countable form
"Nutrients" correctly identifies the specific components of food that provide nutritional value.

32. Many teens today are _____________ to their smartphones and social media. (ADDICT)

Correct answer: addicted

We need an adjective form derived from "ADDICT" to describe the relationship of teens to their devices.

  • "ADDICT" (noun) → "addicted" (adjective) by adding "-ed"
  • Other possible forms: "addiction" (noun), "addictive" (adjective, describing something that causes addiction)
  • "Addicted" is followed by the preposition "to" to indicate what someone is dependent on
"Addicted" correctly describes people who are unable to stop using something as a habit.

33. The _____________ of junk food lies in its high calorie, sugar, and fat content. (DANGEROUS)

Correct answer: danger

We need to convert the adjective "DANGEROUS" to a noun to name the harmful quality.

  • "DANGEROUS" (adjective) → "danger" (noun) by removing "-ous"
  • While "dangerousness" is also a noun form, "danger" is more common and natural in this context
"Danger" is the correct noun form needed in this sentence to describe the harmful aspect of junk food.

34. The garden in my backyard needs to be colorful and _____________. (BEAUTY)

Correct answer: beautiful

We need to form an adjective from the noun "BEAUTY" to describe the desired quality of the garden.

  • "BEAUTY" (noun) → "beautiful" (adjective) by adding "-ful" and adjusting the spelling
  • The sentence requires a second adjective to pair with "colorful"
"Beautiful" is the correct adjective form of "beauty" and pairs naturally with "colorful" to describe the garden.

VII. Rewrite each of the following sentences in another way so that it means almost the same as the sentence printed before it.

37. Smoking is bad for our health. Many people still smoke.

→ ALTHOUGH ____________________________________________

Correct answer: Although smoking is bad for our health, many people still smoke.

This transformation requires using "although" to combine the two sentences with a contrasting relationship:

  • "Although" introduces a clause that contrasts with the main clause
  • The first sentence becomes the subordinate clause introduced by "although"
  • The second sentence becomes the main clause
  • The comma separates the subordinate clause from the main clause
The transformation correctly expresses the contrast between the known health risks of smoking and the fact that people continue to smoke despite this knowledge.

38. Despite feeling tired, Maria went for a jog this morning to stay fit.

→ THOUGH ____________________________________________

Correct answer: Though she was/felt tired, Maria went for a jog this morning to stay fit.

This transformation requires changing "despite" (which is followed by a noun or gerund) to "though" (which is followed by a clause):

  • "Despite feeling tired" must be transformed to a clause with "though"
  • We need to add a subject (she) and change the gerund (feeling) to a verb form (was/felt)
  • The rest of the sentence remains unchanged
The transformation maintains the contrasting relationship between Maria's tiredness and her decision to go jogging.

39. I think you should avoid sugary drinks.

→ SUGGEST ____________________________________________

Correct answer: I suggest (that) you avoid sugary drinks.

This transformation requires changing "I think you should" to "I suggest (that)":

  • "I think you should" is replaced with "I suggest (that)"
  • After "suggest" we can use "that" (which is optional in informal English)
  • The verb after "suggest that" remains in the base form without "should"
The transformation maintains the meaning of giving advice or a recommendation while using "suggest" instead of "think you should."

40. "If I were you, I would schedule regular check-ups with your dentist", she told me.

→ ADVISED ____________________________________________

Correct answer: She advised me to schedule regular check-ups with my dentist.

This transformation requires changing direct speech with a conditional to reported speech using "advised":

  • Direct speech: "If I were you, I would..." becomes "She advised me to..."
  • "Advise" is followed by an object (me) and an infinitive (to schedule)
  • The possessive "your" changes to "my" in reported speech to maintain the reference to the person receiving the advice
The transformation correctly converts the conditional advice in direct speech to reported speech using "advised."
