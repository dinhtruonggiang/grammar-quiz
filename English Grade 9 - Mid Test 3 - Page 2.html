From 1600 to now, people from Britain travelled all around the world and took their language with them. Today, about 375 million people speak English as (19) ________ first language. Another billion people have English as a second language, and perhaps two billion use it in some way, for example, to buy or (20) ________ things or to communicate on the internet.

The internet is, perhaps, the main (21) ________ for the increase in the use of English in the last 20 years. Now, 80% of all communication through the internet is in English, and 95% (22) ________ all articles for science magazines are in English. In most cases, the writers of the articles do not speak English as their first language.

I. Choose the word (A, B, C or D) that best fits each space in the passage:

17.

Correct answer: B. but

The word "but" is used here to show contrast between two ideas. The previous sentence mentions that a language called Englisc existed, but (contrasting idea) only a small number of people spoke it. This creates a logical contrast that needs a conjunction like "but."

18.

Correct answer: C. which

"Which" is the correct relative pronoun to introduce additional information about Britain. The sentence explains that Britain consists of England, Scotland, and Wales. "What" would be used for definitions, "That" typically for defining (rather than non-defining) clauses, and "Where" for locations in a different context.

19.

Correct answer: D. their

The sentence refers to 375 million people speaking English as "their first language." "Their" is the correct possessive adjective to show ownership of the first language by these people. "A" is incorrect as we need a possessive here, "some" doesn't fit grammatically, and "its" would refer to a singular noun.

20.

Correct answer: B. get

In the context "to buy or _____ things," the word "get" forms a natural pair with "buy." When discussing commerce or acquiring items, "buy" and "get" are commonly used together. "Use" doesn't pair naturally with "buy" in this context, "sell" would be the opposite of "buy" (not logical in this sentence), and "have" doesn't fit with the preposition "to" that precedes the blank.

21.

Correct answer: B. reason

The sentence is explaining that the internet is the main cause for the increase in English usage. "Reason" correctly expresses this causal relationship. "Why" is an adverb/question word not a noun, "way" refers to method not cause, and "result" would reverse the cause-effect relationship being described.

22.

Correct answer: A. of

The phrase "95% ___ all articles" requires the preposition "of" to show the relationship between the percentage (95%) and the whole group (all articles). This is the standard way to express percentages in English. None of the other prepositions would be grammatically correct in this context.

II. Use the correct form of the word given in each sentence.

29. Vietnam is home to several ________ wonders that attract people from around the globe. (NATURE)

Correct answer: natural

"Natural" is the adjectival form of "NATURE" and correctly modifies "wonders." We need an adjective here to describe the type of wonders.

30. Visitors to Ha Long Bay are often amazed by its unique rock ________. (FORM)

Correct answer: formations

"Formations" is the noun form of "FORM" that is appropriate in this context. The word refers to naturally occurring arrangements or structures, which perfectly describes the rock structures in Ha Long Bay.

31. They ________ walked down the narrow path in order not to damage the plants growing alongside. (CARE)

Correct answer: carefully

"Carefully" is the adverb form of "CARE" that describes how they walked. An adverb is needed here to modify the verb "walked" and show the manner in which the action was performed.

32. Most of the gifts in the shop were ________, so Jena decided to make her own gift. (COST)

Correct answer: costly

"Costly" is the adjective form of "COST" meaning expensive. The context indicates that Jena decided to make her own gift because the shop's gifts were expensive, so an adjective describing high price is needed.

33. I think taking the overnight train is a better ________, as it allows us to save on accommodation costs. (CHOOSE)

Correct answer: choice

"Choice" is the noun form of "CHOOSE" that refers to the act of selecting or the option selected. Here, we need a noun that follows "a better" to refer to the option of taking the overnight train.

34. The engineer carefully ________ the design to ensure it met all safety standards. (REVIEW)

Correct answer: reviewed

"Reviewed" is the past tense verb form of "REVIEW." The sentence requires a verb in the past tense to match the context and the adverb "carefully" that modifies it.

III. Using the dictionary entry for "INCREASE" to complete the sentences.

increase

verb

• to become greater in amount, number, value, etc.; to make something greater in amount, number, value, etc.

  • Costs have increased significantly
  • to increase dramatically/substantially

increase in something: is used to refer to the amount or level of change within a particular category, variable, or condition. It often highlights the subject or area where the increase is observed.

EX: Oil increased in price.

increase by something: is used to specify the magnitude or amount by which something has increased. It answers the question "how much?" or "by what amount?"

EX: The rate of inflation increased by 2 per cent.

Based on the dictionary entry, the phrase "increase in something" refers to the amount or level of change within a particular category. Here, "sales" is the category where the increase is observed, so "increase in" is the correct phrase.

Correct answer: increase by

According to the dictionary entry, "increase by something" is used to specify the magnitude or amount by which something has increased. Since the sentence specifies "five degrees" as the amount of increase, "increase by" is the correct phrase.

IV. Rewrite each of the following sentences in another way so that it means almost the same as the sentence printed before it.

37. Staying in a beachfront resort is more expensive than staying in a hostel.

→ Staying in a beachfront resort is not

Correct answer: as cheap as staying in a hostel

To rewrite the sentence with "not" while maintaining the same meaning, we need to change the comparative structure "more expensive than" to a negative form of "as...as" comparison. The complete rewritten sentence would be: "Staying in a beachfront resort is not as cheap as staying in a hostel."

38. Mount Elbrus is higher than any other mountains in Russia and Europe.

→ Mount Elbrus is the

Correct answer: highest mountain in Russia and Europe

To rewrite this comparative sentence (higher than any other) using a superlative, we use "the highest." Also, we need to change "mountains" to the singular "mountain" since we're referring to Mount Elbrus as a single entity. The complete rewritten sentence would be: "Mount Elbrus is the highest mountain in Russia and Europe."

39. "I think you should try the delicious street food when you visit Vietnam" Nam said to us.

→ SUGGESTED

Correct answer: Nam suggested (that) we (should) try the delicious street food when we visited Vietnam

To rewrite this direct speech sentence using the word "SUGGESTED," we need to change it to reported speech. The verb "suggested" is followed by a that-clause (the "that" can be omitted). The phrase "should try" can be simplified to just "try" in reported speech. The time reference also changes, so "visit" becomes "visited."

40. "No, I didn't draw on the statue", the young man said.

→ DENIED

Correct answer: The young man denied drawing/having drawn on the statue

To rewrite this direct speech using the word "DENIED," we need to use the structure "deny + gerund (ing form)". The complete rewritten sentence would be: "The young man denied drawing on the statue." An alternative acceptable answer would be: "The young man denied having drawn on the statue."
